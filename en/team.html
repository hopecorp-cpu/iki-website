Knowledge Lineage · 2000+ years of heritage

From ancient Eastern medicine principles to IKI's modern AI framework.

IKI's knowledge base inherits from the essence of traditional Eastern medicine — bridging root principles over 2000 years old to two classical Vietnamese physicians — systematized into a training dataset for the AI Engine.

~3000–200 BCE · Public domain

Yin-Yang · Five Elements

Foundational principles of Eastern medicine

The Yin-Yang theory (Book of Changes, ~3000 BCE) and the Five Elements (Huangdi Neijing, ~200 BCE) — the public heritage of Eastern civilization. These are the core principles from which IKI builds its 5-constitution framework: Cold / Heat / Deficiency / Excess / Damp-phlegm.

14th century · ~1330–1400

Tue Tinh

Founding father of Vietnamese traditional medicine

Author of "Nam Duoc Than Hieu" — the work that laid the foundation for a traditional medicine system built on Vietnamese herbs. Its philosophy, "Vietnamese herbs for Vietnamese people," is the guiding spirit of IKI.

18th century · 1720–1791

Hai Thuong Lan Ong

Le Huu Trac · Great physician of Vietnam

Author of "Hai Thuong Y Tong Tam Linh" — a 28-volume encyclopedia of Vietnamese traditional medicine, distilling knowledge of body constitution, pathology, pharmacology and wellness. A central source of knowledge for IKI's personalization framework.
